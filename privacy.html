Privacy Notice

Last updated: July 8, 2026

Atlas is a macOS app that runs AI agents locally on your own computer. This notice explains what data Atlas collects and what it does with it. It is short, because there isn't much.

What Atlas collects

  • Diagnostic and error reports. When Atlas hits an error or crash, it sends a technical report — error messages, stack traces, app version, and limited surrounding context — to Sentry (a third-party error-monitoring service) and to the developer. This is used only to find and fix bugs.
  • Feedback you send. If you use the in-app feedback feature, the message you write and basic app context (such as the app version) are sent to the developer.
  • Your acceptance of these terms. When you accept the Terms of Use, Atlas records that acceptance — an anonymous install identifier, the terms version, and the date — so there is a record that you agreed. No personal content is included.

What Atlas does not collect

  • Your files, your projects, your prompts, and the contents of accounts you connect (such as Notion, Slack, or email) stay on your computer and in your own accounts. The developer does not receive them — except to the limited extent a fragment might appear inside an error report.
  • Atlas does not sell your data, show ads, or use your data for advertising or profiling.

Third parties

  • Sentry — receives the error and diagnostic reports described above.
  • Anthropic — Atlas's agents run on Claude through your own Claude account and credentials. Your prompts and content are processed by Anthropic under Anthropic's terms; they are not sent to the developer.

Your choices

  • You can ask us to delete diagnostic data associated with you, or ask any question about this notice, by emailing the contact below.
  • Where a setting to disable diagnostic reporting is available in the app, you can turn it off there.
